    Satellite Networks in Computer Networks

    Introduction to Satellite Networks

    Satellite networks form a crucial part of global telecommunications infrastructure, enabling communication across vast distances where terrestrial networks are impractical or impossible to implement.

    Definition

    A satellite network is a wireless network that uses artificial satellites as communication nodes to provide various services including broadcasting, internet access, telephone communications, and military applications.

    Key Components

    • Space Segment (Satellites)
    • Ground Segment (Earth stations)
    • Control Segment (Monitoring systems)

    How Satellite Networks Work

    1. Uplink: Ground station transmits signal to satellite
    2. Transponder: Satellite receives, amplifies, and converts signal
    3. Downlink: Satellite retransmits signal to Earth
    4. Reception: Earth stations or user terminals receive signal

    Types of Satellite Orbits

    Orbit Type Altitude Period Latency Examples
    LEO (Low Earth Orbit) 160-2,000 km 90-120 min 5-20 ms Starlink, Iridium
    MEO (Medium Earth Orbit) 2,000-35,786 km 2-24 hours 50-150 ms GPS, Galileo
    GEO (Geostationary) 35,786 km 24 hours 250-280 ms DirecTV, Intelsat
    HEO (Highly Elliptical) 500-50,000 km 8-24 hours Varies Molniya, Tundra

    LEO Characteristics

    • Low latency ideal for real-time apps
    • Requires constellation of many satellites
    • Short lifespan (5-7 years)

    GEO Characteristics

    • Fixed coverage area
    • High latency unsuitable for VoIP
    • Long lifespan (15+ years)

    Satellite Frequency Bands

    L-band

    1-2 GHz

    Mobile communications, GPS

    C-band

    4-8 GHz

    Weather, TV distribution

    Ku-band

    12-18 GHz

    DTH television, broadband

    Ka-band

    26.5-40 GHz

    High-speed internet

    Frequency Considerations

    • Lower frequencies (L, C bands) have better weather penetration
    • Higher frequencies (Ku, Ka bands) offer more bandwidth but suffer rain fade
    • Frequency allocation is regulated by ITU to prevent interference

    Future Trends in Satellite Networks

    LEO Mega-Constellations

    Companies like SpaceX (Starlink), OneWeb, and Amazon (Project Kuiper) are deploying thousands of small satellites in LEO to provide global broadband coverage with low latency.

    Increased bandwidth Reduced latency Global coverage

    5G Integration

    Satellite networks are being integrated with 5G infrastructure to provide seamless connectivity, especially in remote areas where terrestrial 5G is unavailable.

    Inter-Satellite Links

    Laser communication between satellites creates space-based mesh networks that reduce dependence on ground stations and improve latency.

    Advanced Propulsion

    New propulsion technologies like electric and ion thrusters are extending satellite lifespans and enabling more precise orbital maneuvers.
