AI for Robotics

    Autonomous Vehicles

    Introduction

    Autonomous cars combine robotics, AI, and sensor technology to navigate without human input. These vehicles use machine learning algorithms to process data from various sensors and make real-time driving decisions.

    Levels of Autonomy

    • Level 0: No Automation
    • Level 1: Driver Assistance
    • Level 2: Partial Automation
    • Level 3: Conditional Automation
    • Level 4: High Automation
    • Level 5: Full Automation

    Key Milestones

    • 2004: DARPA Grand Challenge
    • 2009: Google starts self-driving project
    • 2015: First Tesla Autopilot release
    • 2020: Waymo launches driverless taxi service

    Technical Challenges

    Edge Cases

    Handling rare scenarios like construction zones, emergency vehicles, or extreme weather conditions.

    Sensor Limitations

    Cameras struggle with low light, LiDAR is expensive, and radar has limited resolution.

    Ethical Decisions

    The trolley problem: How should the car choose between two bad outcomes?

    Regulatory Hurdles

    Developing unified safety standards and liability frameworks across jurisdictions.

    Future of Autonomous Vehicles

    Short-term (2023-2025)

    • Expansion of robotaxi services in urban areas
    • Level 3 systems in consumer vehicles
    • Improved V2X (vehicle-to-everything) communication

    Medium-term (2025-2030)

    • First fully autonomous consumer vehicles (Level 4)
    • Standardization of autonomous trucking
    • AI-powered traffic management systems

    Long-term (2030+)

    • Ubiquitous autonomous mobility-as-a-service
    • Integration with smart city infrastructure
    • Flying autonomous vehicles
