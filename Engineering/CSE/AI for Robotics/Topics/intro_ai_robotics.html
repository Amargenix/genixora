AI for Robotics

    Introduction to AI for Robotics

    What is AI for Robotics?

    Definition

    AI for Robotics refers to the integration of artificial intelligence techniques with robotic systems to enable autonomous perception, decision-making, and action in dynamic environments.

    It combines:

    • Machine perception (vision, speech, sensors)
    • Cognition (planning, learning, reasoning)
    • Control (motion, manipulation, navigation)

    Key Characteristics

    • Adaptive: Learns from experience
    • Autonomous: Operates independently
    • Interactive: Communicates with humans
    • Purposeful: Goal-directed behavior
    • Robust: Handles uncertainty

    Historical Evolution

    • 1950s-60s: Early robotic automation
    • 1970s-80s: Industrial robotics
    • 1990s: Emergence of behavior-based robotics
    • 2000s: Machine learning integration
    • 2010s-present: Deep learning revolution

    Types of AI Robots

    Industrial Robots

    • Manufacturing: Assembly, welding, painting
    • Logistics: Warehouse automation
    • Quality Control: Inspection systems
    • Key Features: Precision, reliability, speed
    Example: Fanuc's AI-enabled robotic arms learning optimal assembly sequences

    Service Robots

    • Domestic: Vacuuming, lawn mowing
    • Healthcare: Surgical assistants, rehabilitation
    • Retail: Customer service, inventory
    • Key Features: Interaction, adaptability
    Example: SoftBank's Pepper robot engaging with customers in stores

    Autonomous Vehicles

    • Ground: Self-driving cars, delivery robots
    • Aerial: Drones, UAVs
    • Marine: Autonomous ships, submarines
    • Key Features: Navigation, obstacle avoidance
    Example: Waymo's autonomous vehicles navigating complex urban environments

    Humanoid Robots

    • Research: Cognitive development
    • Assistance: Elderly care, disability support
    • Entertainment: Theme parks, exhibitions
    • Key Features: Bipedal locomotion, human-like interaction
    Example: Boston Dynamics' Atlas performing parkour maneuvers
