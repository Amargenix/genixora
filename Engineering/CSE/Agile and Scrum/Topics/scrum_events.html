    The Five Scrum Events

    Scrum events create regularity and minimize the need for meetings not defined in Scrum. All events are time-boxed and designed to enable critical transparency and inspection.

    Detailed Breakdown of Scrum Events

    1 The Sprint

    Definition

    The heartbeat of Scrum where ideas are turned into value. A time-boxed period (typically 1-4 weeks) where a "Done", usable, and potentially releasable product Increment is created.

    Key Aspects

    • Container for all other events
    • Fixed length (no changes during Sprint)
    • Goal is defined in Sprint Planning
    • New Sprint starts immediately after previous concludes

    Sprint Rules

    No Goal Changes: Sprint Goal remains fixed

    Quality Maintained: Definition of Done cannot decrease

    Scope Clarification: Scope may be clarified as learned

    2 Sprint Planning

    Purpose

    To define what can be delivered in the Sprint and how that work will be achieved. Time-boxed to a maximum of 8 hours for a one-month Sprint.

    Topics

    1. Why: Sprint Goal (objective for the Sprint)
    2. What: Product Backlog items selected
    3. How: Plan for delivering selected items

    Outputs

    Sprint Goal

    A cohesive objective that guides the Development Team on why they are building the Increment

    Sprint Backlog

    The set of Product Backlog items selected plus the plan for delivering them

    3 Daily Scrum

    Essentials

    • 15-minute time-boxed event for the Development Team
    • Held at the same time and place every working day
    • Purpose is to inspect progress toward Sprint Goal
    • Not a status meeting but a planning meeting

    Three Questions

    While not mandatory, teams often use these questions:

    1. What did I do yesterday?
    2. What will I do today?
    3. Do I see any impediments?

    Best Practices

    Stand Up: Encourages brevity (not required but common)

    Visual Aids: Use task boards or burn-down charts

    Focus on Flow: Discuss work flow, not just status

    4 Sprint Review

    Objective

    To inspect the Increment and adapt the Product Backlog if needed. Time-boxed to a maximum of 4 hours for a one-month Sprint.

    Activities

    • Demo of "Done" work
    • Review of timeline/budget/scope/capabilities
    • Discussion of what to do next
    • Product Backlog update

    Participants

    Required

    Scrum Team (PO, SM, Dev Team)

    Invited

    Stakeholders, customers, other teams

    Facilitator

    Typically the Product Owner

    5 Sprint Retrospective

    Purpose

    To plan ways to increase quality and effectiveness. The team inspects how the last Sprint went and creates a plan for improvements. Time-boxed to a maximum of 3 hours for a one-month Sprint.

    Focus Areas

    • What went well during the Sprint?
    • What could be improved?
    • Actionable improvement items

    Effective Retro Techniques

    Start/Stop/Continue Mad/Sad/Glad 4Ls (Liked/Learned/Lacked/Longed) Sailboat Timeline

    Scrum Events Interconnection

    Scrum events work together to create a rhythm of regular inspection and adaptation:

    • Sprint Planning kicks off the Sprint
    • Daily Scrum provides daily synchronization
    • Sprint Review inspects the product
    • Sprint Retrospective inspects the process
    • The cycle repeats with each new Sprint

    Key Principles of Scrum Events

    Time-boxing

    Every event has a maximum duration to create regularity and prevent waste.

    Empiricism

    Events enable inspection and adaptation based on real progress.

    Transparency

    All events promote visibility of work, progress, and challenges.

    Value Focus

    Each event serves a specific purpose in delivering customer value.
