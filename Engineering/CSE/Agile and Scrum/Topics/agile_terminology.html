Agile and Scrum

    Agile and Scrum Terminology

    Core Agile Concepts

    Agile Manifesto

    The foundational document for Agile software development with 4 values and 12 principles emphasizing individuals, working software, customer collaboration, and responding to change.

    Iterative Development

    Breaking down projects into small increments with minimal planning, allowing for frequent reassessment and adaptation of plans.

    Incremental Delivery

    Delivering work in small, usable segments rather than complete products in one large release.

    Timeboxing

    Allocating a fixed time period (timebox) to an activity and completing as much as possible within that time.

    Empirical Process Control

    Process based on observation and experimentation (transparency, inspection, adaptation) rather than predefined processes.

    MVP (Minimum Viable Product)

    The version of a product with just enough features to satisfy early customers and provide feedback for future development.

    Scrum Framework Terms

    Roles

    Product Owner

    Responsible for maximizing product value by managing and prioritizing the Product Backlog.

    Scrum Master

    Servant-leader who ensures Scrum is understood and enacted, removing impediments to progress.

    Development Team

    Cross-functional group that delivers potentially releasable increments each Sprint.

    Artifacts

    Product Backlog

    Ordered list of everything needed in the product, maintained by the Product Owner.

    Sprint Backlog

    Set of Product Backlog items selected for the Sprint plus a plan for delivering them.

    Increment

    Sum of all Product Backlog items completed during a Sprint plus all previous Sprints.

    Events

    Sprint

    Time-boxed iteration (typically 1-4 weeks) where work is completed to create a done increment.

    Sprint Planning

    Meeting to determine what can be delivered in the upcoming Sprint and how to achieve it.

    Daily Scrum

    15-minute time-boxed event for the Development Team to synchronize activities.

    Sprint Review

    Informal meeting to inspect the increment and adapt the Product Backlog if needed.

    Sprint Retrospective

    Opportunity for the Scrum Team to inspect itself and create a plan for improvements.

    Common Agile Practices

    User Stories

    Short, simple description of a feature told from the perspective of the user: "As a [role], I want [feature] so that [benefit]."

    Example: "As a customer, I want to save items to a wishlist so I can purchase them later."

    Definition of Done (DoD)

    A shared understanding of what it means for work to be complete, often including coding standards, testing requirements, documentation, etc.

    Burndown Chart

    Graphical representation of work left to do versus time, showing progress toward completing Sprint or release goals.

    Velocity

    A measure of the amount of work a team can complete in a Sprint, used for forecasting future Sprints.

    Backlog Refinement

    Ongoing process of reviewing, updating, and estimating Product Backlog items to prepare for future Sprints.

    Spike

    A time-boxed research activity to gain knowledge needed to reduce uncertainty in estimates or approach.

    Impediment

    Anything that prevents a team member from performing work as efficiently as possible.

    Technical Debt

    Implied cost of additional rework caused by choosing an easy solution now instead of a better approach that would take longer.

    Scaling Agile Terms

    SAFe (Scaled Agile Framework)

    Framework for scaling Agile across large organizations with alignment, collaboration, and delivery across multiple Agile teams.

    LeSS (Large Scale Scrum)

    Framework for scaling Scrum to multiple teams with minimal additional roles and artifacts beyond standard Scrum.

    Nexus

    Scrum.org's framework for scaling Scrum to 3-9 teams working on a single Product Backlog.

    Scrum of Scrums

    Technique to coordinate multiple Scrum teams, typically through representatives meeting regularly.

    Feature Team

    Cross-functional team that can deliver complete customer features rather than working on components.

    PI (Program Increment)

    In SAFe, a timebox (typically 8-12 weeks) during which an Agile Release Train delivers incremental value.

    Agile Estimation Techniques

    Story Points

    Relative measure of effort considering complexity, risk, and uncertainty, often using Fibonacci sequence (1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13).

    Planning Poker

    Consensus-based estimation technique where team members use numbered cards to estimate effort.

    T-Shirt Sizing

    Relative estimation using sizes (XS, S, M, L, XL) for quick, high-level estimation.

    Affinity Estimating

    Grouping similar-sized items together to quickly estimate many items relative to each other.

    Ideal Days

    Estimating work in perfect, uninterrupted work days (not calendar days).

    Bucket System

    Quick estimation method using predefined buckets (ranges) to categorize items.
