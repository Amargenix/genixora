Agile and Scrum

    Scrum of Scrums (SoS)

    What is Scrum of Scrums?

    Scrum of Scrums (SoS) is a scaled agile technique that helps coordinate multiple Scrum teams working on the same product or project. It extends the daily Scrum concept to larger environments where multiple teams need to synchronize their work.

    Purpose

    • Coordinate work across multiple Scrum teams
    • Identify and resolve cross-team dependencies
    • Surface impediments affecting multiple teams
    • Align teams toward common goals

    When to Use

    • Projects with 4+ Scrum teams
    • Complex products with interdependent components
    • Organizations scaling Agile practices
    • When coordination overhead becomes burdensome

    How Scrum of Scrums Works

    Participants

    Each Scrum team selects a representative (often called an "ambassador") to attend the SoS meeting. This is typically:

    • A Scrum Master (most common)
    • A Development Team member
    • Occasionally the Product Owner

    Meeting Structure

    SoS meetings follow a similar structure to daily Scrums but with a broader focus:

    1. Frequency: Typically 2-3 times per week (not necessarily daily)
    2. Duration: 15-30 minutes (timeboxed)
    3. Format: Each representative answers:
      • What has your team done since last meeting?
      • What will your team do before next meeting?
      • What impediments does your team face?
      • Are you about to create any dependencies for other teams?

    Roles in SoS

    SoS Facilitator

    Often a Scrum of Scrums Master (may rotate among team representatives) who ensures the meeting stays focused and productive.

    Team Representatives

    Bring information from their teams and take information back to their teams.

    Product Owner(s)

    May attend to provide product vision clarity and prioritize cross-team issues.

    Benefits of Scrum of Scrums

    Improved Coordination

    Reduces duplication of effort and identifies integration points early.

    Faster Issue Resolution

    Cross-team impediments are surfaced and addressed quickly.

    Better Visibility

    Provides a holistic view of progress across all teams.

    Scaled Communication

    Maintains Agile principles while scaling to larger projects.

    Best Practices for Effective Scrum of Scrums

    • Keep it focused: Stick to cross-team issues, not team-internal matters
    • Rotate facilitators: Prevents knowledge silos and builds shared ownership
    • Document outcomes: Share key decisions and action items with all teams
    • Adjust frequency: More frequent early in sprint, less frequent later
    • Include the right people: Ensure representatives have decision-making authority
    • Follow up: Track action items to completion

    Scrum of Scrums vs. Other Scaling Frameworks

    Framework Approach Best For
    Scrum of Scrums Lightweight coordination between teams Small to medium scaling (4-10 teams)
    SAFe Structured framework with defined roles Large enterprises
    LeSS Minimal rules scaling framework Organizations wanting to stay close to pure Scrum
    Nexus Integration Team coordinates work Scrum.org's scaling approach

    Common Challenges and Solutions

    Challenge: Meeting Bloat

    Solution: Strict timeboxing and focusing only on cross-team issues.

    Challenge: Decision Making

    Solution: Ensure representatives have authority or clear escalation paths.

    Challenge: Information Flow

    Solution: Use visual management tools visible to all teams.

    Challenge: Too Many Teams

    Solution: Consider hierarchical SoS or another scaling framework.

    Implementing Scrum of Scrums

    1. Start Small: Begin with 3-5 teams and expand as needed
    2. Define Purpose: Clearly communicate why you're implementing SoS
    3. Select Representatives: Choose people who can speak for their teams
    4. Establish Cadence: Determine optimal meeting frequency
    5. Create Feedback Loops: Regularly assess SoS effectiveness
    6. Integrate with Events: Align with sprint planning/reviews
    7. Use Visual Management: Make dependencies visible to all

    Scrum of Scrums is a practical approach to scaling Agile that maintains the core Scrum values while addressing the coordination challenges of multiple teams working on the same product.
