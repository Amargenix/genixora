    Velocity and Burndown in Agile and Scrum

    Velocity

    Velocity is a metric that measures how much work a Scrum team completes during a sprint, typically expressed in story points or ideal days. It's calculated by summing the estimates of all completed backlog items at the end of the sprint.

    Burndown Chart

    A burndown chart is a graphical representation of work left to do versus time. It shows the rate at which work is being completed and how much work remains, helping teams predict when all work will be completed.

    Understanding Velocity

    Calculating Velocity

    Velocity = Sum of story points for all completed user stories in a sprint

    Example: If a team completes:

    • Story A (5 points)
    • Story B (3 points)
    • Story C (2 points)

    Velocity = 10 points

    Uses of Velocity

    • Forecast how much work can be done in future sprints
    • Identify trends in team performance
    • Help with release planning
    • Improve sprint planning accuracy
    • Assess impact of process changes

    Velocity Tracking Best Practices

    • Use 3-5 sprints to establish baseline
    • Only count fully completed stories
    • Don't compare velocities across teams
    • Review velocity trends, not single points
    • Adjust for known anomalies (holidays, team changes)

    Understanding Burndown Charts

    Types of Burndown Charts

    Sprint Burndown

    Tracks remaining work in current sprint (daily updates)

    Release Burndown

    Tracks remaining work toward a release (updated per sprint)

    Key Elements

    • X-axis: Time (days or sprints)
    • Y-axis: Remaining work (story points or hours)
    • Ideal line: Straight line from total work to zero
    • Actual line: Shows real progress
    • Scope changes: Vertical adjustments when work is added/removed

    Interpreting Patterns

    • Above ideal: Team is behind schedule
    • Below ideal: Team is ahead of schedule
    • Flat line: No progress being made
    • Upward spikes: Scope creep (work added)
    • Steep drops: Large chunks completed at once

    Best Practices for Using Velocity and Burndown

    Velocity Do's

    • Use for capacity planning
    • Track trends over time
    • Review during retrospectives
    • Adjust for team changes

    Velocity Don'ts

    • Don't use as performance metric
    • Don't compare across teams
    • Don't expect linear growth
    • Don't game the system

    Burndown Tips

    • Update daily for sprint charts
    • Make visible to entire team
    • Discuss deviations in standups
    • Use for early warning signs

    Common Challenges and Solutions

    Challenge Possible Causes Solutions
    Erratic Velocity Inconsistent estimation, changing team size, varying story sizes Establish estimation baseline, normalize story sizes, track team changes
    Burndown Flatlines Blockers not resolved, work not being updated, tasks too large Address blockers daily, break down tasks, ensure daily updates
    Velocity Declines Technical debt, morale issues, unclear requirements Address tech debt, improve refinement, check team health
    Burndown Spikes Up Scope creep, underestimated work, discovery of new work Enforce scope discipline, improve estimation, capture all work
