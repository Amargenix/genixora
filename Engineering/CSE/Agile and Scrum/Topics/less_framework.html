    LeSS Framework in Agile and Scrum

    What is LeSS Framework?

    Large-Scale Scrum (LeSS) is a framework for scaling agile development to multiple teams. LeSS builds on top of standard single-team Scrum by maintaining its core principles while providing framework elements for applying Scrum at scale with minimal additional rules.

    LeSS Flavors

    • Basic LeSS: For 2-8 teams (up to 50 people)
    • LeSS Huge: For 8+ teams (50+ people)
    • LeSS Experiments: Various organizational experiments

    LeSS Principles

    • More with LeSS: Less roles, artifacts, processes
    • Whole Product Focus: Feature teams over component teams
    • Customer-Centric: Focus on real customer needs
    • Continuous Improvement: Kaizen mindset

    LeSS vs. Single-Team Scrum

    LeSS maintains Scrum's simplicity while scaling:

    Scrum Element LeSS Adaptation
    Product Owner One PO for all teams (Basic LeSS)
    Sprint Planning Two parts: What + How (multi-team coordination)
    Daily Scrum Optional cross-team coordination after team standups
    Sprint Review Single combined review for all teams

    Key LeSS Roles

    Product Owner

    Single PO for all teams (Basic LeSS) or Area PO in LeSS Huge. Responsible for the entire product backlog.

    Scrum Master

    Focuses on the overall system and organizational impediments rather than individual teams.

    Feature Team

    Cross-functional, cross-component team that can deliver complete customer-centric features.

    LeSS Events

    1. Overall Sprint Planning: Part 1 (What) with all teams together
    2. Team Sprint Planning: Part 2 (How) per team
    3. Overall Retrospective: After team retrospectives to address systemic issues
    4. Multi-team Daily Scrum: Optional coordination after team standups
    5. Overall Sprint Review: Single integrated review with all stakeholders
    6. Product Backlog Refinement: Often done in multi-team workshops

    LeSS Huge Specifics

    Requirement Areas

    Product is divided into Requirement Areas (RAs), each with its own Area Product Owner and teams.

    Area Product Owners

    Each Requirement Area has an APO who works under the Chief Product Owner.

    Coordination

    Additional coordination mechanisms like Area PO Sync and Overall Retrospective.

    Benefits of LeSS

    • Maintains Scrum's simplicity at scale
    • Reduces organizational complexity
    • Promotes feature teams over component teams
    • Encourages whole-product focus
    • Minimizes additional roles and artifacts
    • Improves transparency across teams
    • Reduces dependencies between teams
    • Focuses on delivering customer value

    LeSS Adoption Tips

    1. Start Small

    Begin with 2-3 teams before scaling to LeSS Huge if needed.

    2. Train Everyone

    Ensure all team members understand Scrum and LeSS principles.

    3. Form Feature Teams

    Transition from component to feature teams gradually.

    4. Simplify Structure

    Remove unnecessary roles and layers in the organization.
