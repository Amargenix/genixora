    Scrum Ceremonies in Agile

    The Four Scrum Ceremonies

    Scrum ceremonies (also called events) are structured meetings that create regularity and minimize the need for other meetings. Each ceremony serves a specific purpose in the Scrum framework.

    Detailed Breakdown of Scrum Ceremonies

    1 Sprint Planning

    Purpose

    To define what can be delivered in the Sprint and how that work will be achieved. Time-boxed to 8 hours for a one-month Sprint (shorter for shorter Sprints).

    Participants

    • Entire Scrum Team (Dev Team, PO, SM)
    • Optional: Stakeholders for clarification

    Key Outputs

    Sprint Goal

    A clear objective that guides the team on why they are building the Increment

    Sprint Backlog

    The set of Product Backlog items selected plus the plan for delivering them

    Best Practices

    Preparation: Product Backlog should be refined before planning

    Collaboration: Whole team participates in creating the plan

    Realism: Commit only to what can realistically be done

    2 Daily Scrum

    Essentials

    • 15-minute time-boxed event for the Development Team
    • Held at the same time and place every working day
    • Purpose is to inspect progress toward Sprint Goal
    • Not a status meeting but a planning meeting

    Three Questions

    While not mandatory, teams often use these questions:

    1. What did I do yesterday to help meet the Sprint Goal?
    2. What will I do today to help meet the Sprint Goal?
    3. Do I see any impediments?

    Advanced Techniques

    Walk the Board

    Discuss work items moving across the task board from right to left

    Impediment Parking Lot

    Track blockers separately for follow-up after the meeting

    Swarming

    Identify items needing multiple team members' attention

    3 Sprint Review

    Objective

    To inspect the Increment and adapt the Product Backlog if needed. Time-boxed to 4 hours for a one-month Sprint.

    Typical Agenda

    1. Product Owner explains what's "Done" and what's not
    2. Development Team demonstrates the work
    3. Group discusses product progress and market changes
    4. Product Backlog adjustments
    5. Review timeline, budget, and potential capabilities

    Participants

    • Required: Scrum Team
    • Invited: Stakeholders, customers, other teams
    • Facilitator: Typically the Product Owner

    Demo Tips

    • Show working software (not slides)
    • Focus on business value delivered
    • Prepare test data/scenarios in advance
    • Keep it interactive and engaging

    4 Sprint Retrospective

    Purpose

    To plan ways to increase quality and effectiveness. The team inspects how the last Sprint went and creates a plan for improvements. Time-boxed to 3 hours for a one-month Sprint.

    Focus Areas

    • What went well during the Sprint?
    • What could be improved?
    • Actionable improvement items

    Participants

    • Development Team
    • Scrum Master
    • Product Owner
    • No stakeholders (internal team meeting)

    Output

    • Identified improvements
    • Action plan for next Sprint
    • Owner(s) for each action item

    Retrospective Techniques

    Start/Stop/Continue Mad/Sad/Glad 4Ls (Liked/Learned/Lacked/Longed) Sailboat Timeline Speed Car Starfish Lean Coffee

    Ceremony Timing Guidelines

    Ceremony 1-Week Sprint 2-Week Sprint 4-Week Sprint
    Sprint Planning 2 hours 4 hours 8 hours
    Daily Scrum 15 min 15 min 15 min
    Sprint Review 1 hour 2 hours 4 hours
    Sprint Retrospective 45 min 1.5 hours 3 hours

    Common Ceremony Anti-Patterns

    Sprint Planning

    • PO dictates the plan
    • No Sprint Goal created
    • Over-commitment
    • Ignoring team capacity

    Daily Scrum

    • Turning into status report
    • SM running the meeting
    • Solving problems during meeting
    • Not focusing on Sprint Goal

    Sprint Review

    • Showing slides instead of working software
    • Not inviting stakeholders
    • No Product Backlog updates
    • Turning into a demo-only session

    Retrospective

    • Skipping it when time is short
    • No action items generated
    • Same format every time
    • Not addressing tough issues
